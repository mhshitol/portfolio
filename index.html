01. About Me

My story starts at Daffodil International University, where I studied Software Engineering with a major in Data Science. Somewhere between my first machine learning course and my final thesis, I got hooked on one question: can we teach machines to spot diseases earlier than we can?

Chasing that question led to an IEEE-published framework for diabetic retinopathy detection, a thesis on retinal disease prediction, and research projects in explainable AI, computer vision, and healthcare systems. When a model makes a call, I want to know why — that's the thread running through all of it.

Away from research, I like shipping things people can actually click on — task apps, chatbots, pet shop systems. My team's surveillance drone once took 2nd runner-up at an international conference, and NASA Space Apps 2025 called us "Galactic Problem Solvers," which still makes me smile.
